the day before

i arrived on campus at 10:30am, and i went to the banana lounge to play nomifactory (ceu 1.7a) until someone else arrived.

soon, adal and zimodo came over. i decided to download gtnh and we looked through its questbook in a creative world just in case there was a puzzle about it. most of it (up until uhv ish) was stuff i was decently familiar with, but we did find a few silly things such as the "you're gonna hate this" quest series.

while this was happening, adal was building a periodic table out of a bag of big and small bananagrams tiles that just happened to be on the table we were sitting at. we first tried making the big tiles the uppercase letters and the small tiles the lowercase letters, but that was only enough for about a quarter of the table, so we instead decided to just focus on the uppercase letters. however, we still had not enough of the letters that we wanted, so we started doing shenanigans such as using u's turned sideways for extra c's.

afterwards, we went to le meridien in an attempt to meet more people. the only people we met there were a few people from cardinality whose names i have mostly forgotten already. since adal was wearing his birdsuit (which was red), and since cardinality was eventually the first team to finish, this interaction was to be the first of many times people thought he was affiliated with cardinality.

friday

i went to flour for the now traditional puzzlers club meetup. (note: puzzlers club is distinct from mit puzzle club) the size of the meetup has grown from 17 in 2023 to 33 last year to 36 this year.

then i went to a different flour for the traditional teammate meetup. we were planning to walk to the kickoff room in building 10 (kickoff was in 3 distinct locations this year since kresge was under maintenance). when we entered the room, a bunch of people decided to take pictures of 6 pieces of paper that were taped to the wall, thinking that they were a puzzle. it turned out that they weren't, and i didn't worry about them at all since i saw them at the how to hunt workshop on thursday.

kickoff was pretty good, they projected a video instead of performing a live skit because kickoff was in 3 different rooms. i really liked the neurodivergent person (ferdinand) and the lesbians (katrina and baby). the plot also transitioned into the hunt very well.

afterwards, we went to our rooms. i decided to go check out the gala, and i saw that d&m wasn't letting anyone in until the hunt actually started. this was also partially to find a short path between the gala and our room, and i found out that using the bridges on the third floor was the best choice.

the unlock structure worked a lot differently this year than previous years. in previous years, we were just given puzzles that unlocked in a strict order. now, we had a set number of keys to unlock puzzles with, and a few more puzzles that we could see a description of but couldn't look at directly until we unlocked them. i liked this design since it meant that we can selectively unlock puzzles that we knew we would work on, and have a larger number of puzzles that we could know about without getting overwhelmed. the main issue would probably have been coordinating these unlocks between teams, but teammate just chose 3 people to give the exclusive authority to unlock puzzles. i think this worked mainly since everyone trusted them to make the correct decisions, which may not be the case for other teams.

i decided to sit near justin yokota, to whom i mentioned that we'd probably be working on the same puzzles a lot of the time. early on, he had the idea of giving ferdinand a croissant at the gala and seeing what they had to say about it. we didn't know it was actually puzzle content, so the response we did get when we did it was kinda disappointing.

sunday

when i saw the answer to alias i was hoping that said something about ferdinand being trans, but it turns out that shirley was not a particularly gendered name.

also, w i d e f e r d i n a n d

this was around the time cardinality finished, so some people from cardinality that knew us well came over to spectate us. we jokingly asked them for answers, and i think they just gave us encouragement.

we solved the shell corporations in this order (underlined numbers are the ones we solved, arrows are what puzzle we used to backsolve):

  • 1
  • 3
  • 7 <- 1
  • 4 <- 7
  • 6
  • 8
  • 2 <- 8
  • 5

1 was solved on saturday night, missing two of the feeders. 3 was solved slightly later on saturday night but i didn't really look at it.

on sunday afternoon, we found that penrose was a good feeder for 1. it didn't work for any of the feeders, but we tried it on some of the other shell corporations and 7 worked.

7 wasn't too hard to figure out the mechanics of, so we backsolved that to get that it used 1's answer plus acrylate, which was the correct answer to 4.

afterwards, i spent a while looking at 8. initially we ran into a lot of contradictions since we did not have any strings containing a state whose animal was either a moose or a deer that was also compatible with the numbers. we had to wait for more feeders to get solved.

then, 6 was solved. the answer it gave fit into our grid perfectly, and the remaining squares that didn't have letters had a unique path through them. i was able to gradually wheel of fortune through the letters of those squares, and picked up the answer to 2.

finally, 5 got solved. then we unlocked the metameta.

we took a while to notice that we were supposed to remove the first letter from each word, but afterwards, justin and i started working on the logic puzzle, in parallel with some other teammates. we broke it since we missed an edge while drawing the graph.

finally, we were crowding around the finished diagram when we got to the final step of marking the edges of the extra vertex, and we kept being rate limited by making guesses when we had half of the letters that we needed. this was a great experience though since i saw (and also contributed to) the deductions getting worked out in real time.

this was the only round where i worked on the final meta, but i was very fortunate that it happened to be this round. the round was gimmicked, and the exact mechanic of the gimmick was relevant to solving the metameta. i really liked the process of realizing that this was how the metas in this round worked, even though it did take a while for us to make progress on metas despite figuring this out.

i definitely have a preference for unconventional (or what teammate calls "illegal") puzzles. this can also be shown by the fact that my favorite puzzles of all time are gph 2023 and abcdefg final metametas, and  ship.

my preferences for going against conventions also extends to outside puzzles. the play-by-post (asynchronous) games of spirit island that i host tend to be gimmicked in a way that disrupts the main strategies for many spirits. this even extends to real life, where i find someone a lot more interesting after finding out that they're lgbtq+ and even more if they're trans.

we finished the last round remaining, murder in mitropolis, around the same time we finished the paper trail round, at around 6pm. hq scheduled an interaction with us at 7pm, so we just socialized for an hour. then we went to the gala.

i think it was kinda funny to see the entirety of teammate walk through the gala, though i don't know if anyone else was paying attention to the fact that the entire team was there. i decided that i wanted to skip any interactions before the runaround, since i wanted to talk to people at the gala, until my friends from mit puzzle club urged me to go to the runaround because it was starting soon.

i didn't actually participate much in this, i just watched the rest of my team line up, each holding an item to make a sound. there were a few funny interactions here, such as with the chickens, and someone singing this song when the radio was playing.

finally, we were let into the vault, and the finale of the plot happened.

afterwards, i returned to the gala to continue talking to people from mit puzzle club. it was pretty clear that we were the third team to finish, after cardinality and providence, and the next two teams were nes and super team awesome in some order. a bunch of the other strong teams still had a couple metas unfinished, and there were only two hours until the gala closed, which means that they would need to finish all the metas soon if they wante a chance to do the runaround.

if i had known where other teams' rooms were, i may have visited them that night.

monday

i again went to campus early and stayed in the banana lounge playing nomifactory until around 11. i decided to visit galactic's assigned room at around 11 to socialize a bit. yannick was there touching up the tests for integration bee which was on tuesday, and there were a few others cleaning up. there were a lot of people on galactic that i've never met in person, but i recognized some of them despite having never seen their faces.

wrapup didn't have that much for me to write about, since it was mostly about puzzles i didn't work on. i did end up finding out what the literal animal farm copypasta was at the end, and i think not knowing what it is makes it funnier.

after wrapup, we decided that the more-recently-traditional post-wrapup mit puzzle club meeting was going to happen on tuesday, so i no longer had plans for the afternoon. i was pretty indecisive so i walked to happy lamb with a bunch of people from teammate and then crossed the street to get to clover at the last minute. i was planning to go to pandemonium afterwards, but i accidentally got caught up with flirting with people.

reflections

on tuesday, we discussed the future of mit puzzle club, and it sounds like it will flourish in the upcoming years, as long as the running team still has enough mit students and alumni that know what they're doing. we might even be able to do even more stuff next year than this year. there also hasn't been any discourse:tm: on mystery hunt in general this year, which is a good sign. i'm expecting providence to win in the next couple years, and i believe in their ability to keep mystery hunt running well.

it feels like the club just does better when i stop being part of its leadership, though i think completing the recovery from covid is the bigger reason for its flourishing.

the much earlier first finish this year was very good, but it might have been better to have the second place finish not 8 hours later. hopefully next year's hunt will have the coin found at a similar time, or even earlier. (sunday morning is my preferred time for the coin to be found)

i really liked the gala, as it gave a good channel for socializing with people from other teams. however, i was kinda too caught up on solving puzzles to go there often. i do kinda wish i had made more visits. there was also a photo booth that i didn't use at all.

i never really interacted with the radio, but its existence was quite cool, especially as a physical component that could have been used for a lot of different things. my main amusement was when two weather reports in a row were interrupted by the puzzle solved jingle. i also liked that it played music, as i like listening to music while i work (which may not actually make me more productive, i just like it)

i find that i always have a desire to write more puzzles right after every mystery hunt. let's call that andriddline. my andriddline this year was strong enough to make me consider joining cardinality to write again, but there might be enough other hunts to write for that can satiate all of that andriddline, and i'm pretty sure it also fades over time. in fact, andriddline was the reason i joined galactic to write for gph in 2023, and its fading was why i didn't actually write any puzzles in gph 2023.

i keep getting crushes on people that i meet at mystery hunt. i have no idea how to fix this or whether this is something that warrants fixing.
